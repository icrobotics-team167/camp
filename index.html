ABOUT

The LEGO® Robotics summer camp is an exploration into robotics. Students will delve into different aspects of autonomous machines — design, construction, programming, and problem-solving. Mentors and high-school volunteers from FIRST® Robotics Competition Team 167 and FIRST® Tech Challenge Team 8696 will lead the camp.

No experience is required in robotics. Camp instructors will provide a comprehensive course in beginner robotics using LEGO® Mindstorms. At the end of the week, students will be able to complete various challenges such as a maze, a battle-bots simulation, and tug-of-war.

For students that are interested in continuing robotics, FIRST® LEGO League Jr. and FIRST® LEGO League are good options after this camp. Once students reach high school, they can opt to join FIRST® Tech Challenge or FIRST® Robotics Competition.

ABOUT

The Iowa City Robotics programming summer camp introduces students to fundamental computer science concepts. Students will learn to apply problem-solving skills to overcome programming challenges and eventually create simple text-based games.

The activities offered in this camp will cover many of the same concepts present in the AP Computer Science course taught at the high-school level. Students participating in this camp will have the opportunity to gain some early exposure to these concepts in preparation for AP Computer Science should they choose to take that course later.

Students are not expected to have any prior experience with programming or discrete mathematics, but students that do have prior experience are also encouraged to take part in the camp. Computers and other equipment will be supplied by the team.
